The rule: starting from any Wikipedia article, take the first link in the body text — ignoring parentheses, italics, and anything outside the main prose — then repeat on the page you land on. Do that enough times and, famously, roughly 19 times in 20 you end up at Philosophy, which loops back on itself.

This visualization either crawls that rule live against Wikipedia's API from your browser, or loads a synthetic demo web built from real article titles arranged to show the same converging shape (clearly not live link data). Each chain becomes a branch; branches that share a page merge, which is why it fans out like a web instead of separate threads.

Controls: drag to orbit, scroll to zoom, shift-drag (or right-drag) to pan, click a node to inspect it and trace its path to Philosophy, search to jump to any loaded page. Use the Hops shown slider to expand or contract the depth of the visible web.
